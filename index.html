User, Product and Competitive Research

What is it that GoFar does?

On-board diagnostics (OBD) is an automotive term referring to a vehicle's ability of self-diagnostic and reporting. GoFar connects your mobile phone with your engine so it can give the vehicle owner or repair technician access to the status of the various vehicle subsystems. It can also give you instant feedback on the way you drive helping you save money.

Who is their current target audience?

They want to reach as many people as possible when they first launch, so they thought about the normal consumers. Although the idea seems great we found that it could be improved.

Who & how do people use their mobile inside the car?

A online survey with 53 responses gave us great insight into what was needed for an ideal mobile app.

...we had responses from all over the world.

Our responses came from Latin America, USA, Europe, Oceania and Asia, from engineers to babysitters.

Who competes with GoFar?

We had a heuristic look at competitors sites to see what was on offer.

There are not many good competitors in Sydney.

We researched the competitors online and found that most competitors are overseas.

How do people want to receive instant feedback?

33.9% prefered voice and 30.4% prefered sound.

But those two were the main user pain points about the competitors.

We created personas.

We created 5 user groups that covered the majority of users we had found in the research stage.

The Magic Toy Shop

Brief

Based in Balmain, The Magic Toy Shop wanted a online store to show all their products while maintaining their brand image of tradition, fun and creativity.

Client

The Magic Toy Shop is a traditional toy shop. This client was part of a project provided by General Assembly.

Duration

2 weeks

Team

  • Juanny A. Pereira

My Approach

User and competitor research, personas, sketching, site map, wireframing, prototyping, testing, iterating and presenting.

Tools

AAxure, interviews, keynote, sharpies, sticky notes & paper..

User and competitor research

What was asked

TMTS wanted to show their products and give the users a pleasant journey while they were using their website. Their audience was very broad so they needed their items to be easily find. They also wanted to promote the vintage toys. They were a very characteristic point of the business. They wanted to give the user the opportunity to review their purchases and create the sense of community. Also, separating the toys by gender wasn't an option.

How is the current selling system?

I analysed their current selling system and identified their characteristic points. Because, at the end, they still wanted the user to feel that their purchase was customised. I found several points that could be transferred to an online system.

Personas

I was given 3 personas. Each of them had different goals with the store.
User Pain Points
Identifying their pain points was an essential step because in the future it would help me deciding which feature were better and which ones were not.

Who competes with The Magic Toy Shop?

I had a heuristic look at competitors sites to see what was on offer and found that most of the websites didn't cover what TMTS needed. In most cases the search was very confusing and only few of them sold vintage toys.

Opportunity!

There was a good space in the market for TMTS to create an innovative and unique e-commerce model.

Products

I was given a list of TMTS existent products so the next step of the research were an online card sorting to see how the users would categorise the large range of toys.

ClimbMe

Brief

Zoe wants to go climb and know all the information she needs to know without having to carry massive heavy books.

Client

Zoe - General Assembly class colleague.

Duration

1 weeks

Team

  • Juanny A. Pereira

My Approach

Problem statement, user research, , personas, sketching, app map, testing, iterating and presenting.

Tools

Keynote, Sharpies, paper and Sketch.

User & Competitor Research

Kick Off Meeting

In the first day of the project we had a kick-off meeting so Zoe presented me her problem. I handled the project with an Agile approach, so we we had daily meetings. Finding reliable and straightforward information about rocks and routes is difficult. Websites are most of the times not updated frequently so if the conditions change, she can't know.

The books are too heavy.

More reliable info could be found in books but they have too much information which isn't needed. So she needed something she could easily access on and offline.

What was the type of information that she needed?

Before her activity she needs to know 5 key informations, so I made them the MVP of the project.
How she wanted to access the information?
After some interviews, mobile platform was proved to be more useful for he. She wanted a very simple and easy to access app.

Information Architecture

User Flow & Scenarios

I created 2 scenarios where Zoe would use the app: at home, in the wild. My colleague and the user research I did with her gave me the confirmation about what was nee needed to create the ideal user flows. I sketched and brainstormed many iterations of user flows. We iterated many times with the aim of trying to simplify the process where possible.

Sketching

How do I create something combining what Zoe wants and what she needs? After every change, every single step, I went back to my user to iterate and receive feedback. With sharpies and paper I then sketched low fi app screens, re-iterated and tested.

Testing, Iterating & Presenting.

I tested the early wireframes and iterated... The early wireframes required a few changes after testing with user. After that, the wireframe was completed ready to show to the client.
